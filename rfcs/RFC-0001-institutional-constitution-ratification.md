# RFC-0001: Institutional Constitution Ratification

**Status**: Accepted  
**Type**: Historical Record  
**Date**: 2026-07-23

## 1. Summary

This RFC records the decision to ratify Institutional Constitution v0.1 for v0.x exploratory use and to establish the constitutional baseline of the Epistemic Civilization framework.

It does not reopen architectural debate. It preserves provenance.

## 2. Decision

On 2026-07-23 the Institutional Constitution v0.1 was accepted as the sole source of institutional legitimacy for the framework. The document was frozen under the rule that subsequent amendments require identification of a concrete ambiguity, inconsistency, or governance failure.

## 3. Rationale

The Constitution cleanly separates institutional legitimacy and governance from all computational and implementation concerns. It establishes:

- Three Constitutional Laws (Governed Knowledge, Replaceable Implementations, Replayable Evolution)
- Guiding principles including preservation of uncertainty and the rule that observation is open while commitment is gated
- Four Constitutional States (Observed, Provisional, Institutional, Historical)
- Ratification Gates with authority-based language
- A clear relationship to subordinate specifications (CEK, EIS, RC, RPS)

These decisions had already been reached through extended architectural refinement. This RFC exists solely to make the ratification event itself attributable and replayable.

## 5. Superseded Material

All prior conversational drafts of the Institutional Constitution are superseded by the ratified text. No earlier draft retains normative force.

## 6. Consequences

- All subsequent specifications MUST remain consistent with Institutional Constitution v0.1.
- The project itself now operates under constitutional governance.
- Exploratory design continues in RFCs; normative specifications incorporate only Accepted decisions.

## 7. Status

**Accepted** on 2026-07-23. This RFC is closed to further debate.
